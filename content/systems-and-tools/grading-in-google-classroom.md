# Grading in Google Classroom

## General

There are a bunch of different ways to grade assessments on Google Classroom. This will only cover one common way. Whichever way you choose, grading in Google Classroom includes the following steps:

### Accessing the Assignments

1. Go to the sub nav item “To review” link under “Teaching” nav item.
2. Filter the class you want to grade. Google Classroom shows all the assessments of all Google Classrooms you’re part of.
3. Select the assessment you want to grade.
   - “Turned in” shows the assessments needing grading - this is the most important indicator.
   - “Assigned” shows the number of students that have access to a specific assessment.
   - “Graded” shows assessments that have been fully graded.

### Assigning a Mark to the Assignments

- Look through the learner’s submissions against the instructions.
- Assign a mark to a specific learner.

### Returning or Finalizing Assignments

Once a mark has been assigned, it will usually show as a draft, in green. You need to return it to the learner to finalize it. This is to say you’re done giving feedback and the learner can resubmit according to the feedback or leave it as is if no revisions are required.

- Optionally mark the assignment as reviewed to avoid seeing it on the list of “To review” assignments.

## Grading by Assignment Type

### Complete or Incomplete Type Assignments

These are the assignments where you’re checking for completion of a specific resource that can be proved (e.g., FreeCodeCamp, SoloLearn). The students usually submit a link to their publicly accessible profile. You then have to open the link and check whether it’s complete or not.

> Google Classroom does not have a built-in way of saying whether an assignment is complete or not. It works on a point system. So here’s a recommendation:

- For Complete assignments: Award these **100 points**.
- For Incomplete assignments: Award these **0 points**.

### Codio Assignments

#### Grading Codio Assignments

Make sure you have each module’s release grades feature turned on on Codio so that the results can be pulled through as final on Google Classroom.

- For auto graded assignments: You have nothing to worry about. As long as the release grades slider above has been turned on for the modules you want to get final grades from.
- For manually graded assignments: See Internal Team Training.

#### Resetting Student Projects for Resubmissions

Sometimes learners do so badly in their Codio projects that you have to reset the projects and give them another chance.

### General Flow to Follow

1. Look through the Codio grades tab and find where the students have gotten unsatisfactory marks.
2. Go to the matching submissions on Codio and reset it so that they can be able to redo their submissions.
3. Send them a private note on the assignment in question on Google Classroom. The note should state that they should redo the assignment because of a low grade.
