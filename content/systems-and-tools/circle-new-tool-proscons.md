# Circle: New Tool! Pros/Cons

## Identify the Need

### What is the business need? What problem are you trying to solve?
- Have a place to communicate with learners in private groups defined by cohort, partner, club, year, etc.
- Have a space where learners can communicate with one another and share ideas/resources/get to know one another virtually.
- *Own* our entire community in one space.
- Have persistence and permissions of the community space agnostic of particular staff members.
- Regulate/review the communications any staff/learner member may be having with learner spaces.
- Ensure that the space is secure even if a staff member leaves.
- Centralise all resources, the one-stop-shop for community engagement, virtual campus.
- Staff well-being and privacy.
- No personal phone numbers are accessible by learners.
- Having public groups/externally facing to grow our community base and potential partners.
- Members index.
- DMs/Announcements.
- Inclusive space that's not intimidating.

### In the future, how could this potentially shift?
- If we collect email addresses, we're future-proofing this to migrate to almost any other platform.
- If we collect phone numbers and wait till WhatsApp gets better with permissions/privacy, then we can possibly nurture that too, or switch WhatsApp to being more announcements oriented.

## Evaluate Options

### Tool Selection Checklist
- Could this tool solve any additional business needs?
  - Live Stream
  - Light weight LMS
  - Video resource library
  - FYI knowledge base / FAQs
  - Job board
  - Forum

### What other tools do you use?
- RocketChat
- Slack

### Does it integrate with other tools?
- Yes

### Will an "off the shelf" method work, or do you need something more custom? If so, how easy will that be to create?
- How easy is it to work with their customer support? How much help can you expect from them during setup and use?
- What is the cost now?
  - Consider initial acquisition costs, ongoing maintenance, and potential hidden costs.
- How does that cost scale as you add more people?
- What does the tool's feature roadmap look like? Will it be improved over time?

## Pilot Testing
- Before fully committing to a tool, consider running a small-scale pilot test. This will help identify any potential issues or limitations and provide insights into its practicality.
- Collect user feedback and consider the feedback, needs, and concerns in the decision-making process.

## Make a Decision
- Based on the research, evaluations, cost-benefit analysis, and feedback, select the tool that best aligns with Umuzi's needs and objectives.

## Implementation Plan
