# Custom Design Thinking & MUB GPTs

## Overview
The Design Thinking GPT Series (Days 1–5) is a set of five specialized AI assistants designed to guide participants through the complete Design Thinking process. Each GPT corresponds to one day of a structured 5-day workshop and is customized to deliver content, exercises, and support appropriate to that day’s focus. Together, these GPTs simulate a progressive design sprint, helping users move from empathy and problem definition to prototyping and testing, while maintaining continuity across sessions.

## How to Use the Series
- Start with Day 1 and progress sequentially to Day 5.
- Each GPT assumes the previous day’s work has been completed.
- Users can paste notes or summaries from the prior day to maintain context.

## GPT Breakdown

### Day 1 — Empathize
**Focus:** Understanding the user and their needs.

**Key Activities:**
- Conduct user interviews and empathy mapping
- Identify pain points and motivations
- Analyze findings with “How Might We…” questions

**GPT Capabilities:**
- Generates empathy maps and personas
- Suggests interview questions
- Summarizes user insights into clear themes

### Day 2 — Define
**Focus:** Synthesizing research into a clear problem statement.

**Key Activities:**
- Cluster insights using affinity mapping
- Write user-centered problem statements
- Identify the core challenge to solve

**GPT Capabilities:**
- Helps reframe challenges
- Validates if a problem statement is “actionable and human-centered”
- Guides users through creating POV (Point of View) statements

### Day 3 — Ideate
**Focus:** Generating a wide range of creative solutions.

**Key Activities:**
- Brainstorming, SCAMPER, “Crazy 8s” exercises
- Idea clustering and prioritization
- Selection of promising ideas for prototyping

**GPT Capabilities:**
- Facilitates ideation sessions
- Suggests brainstorming prompts
- Organizes ideas into themes or feasibility categories

### Day 4 — Prototype
**Focus:** Bringing ideas to life through rapid experimentation.

**Key Activities:**
- Low-fidelity prototyping (sketches, storyboards, mockups)
- Planning how to test prototypes with users
- Gathering early feedback

**GPT Capabilities:**
- Guides the choice of prototype format (digital, physical, story)
- Helps outline prototype components and test scripts
- Suggests tools and methods for quick iteration

### Day 5 — Test
**Focus:** Learning from user interactions with the prototype.

**Key Activities:**
- Conduct usability testing
- Record user feedback and observations
- Identify learnings and improvements

**GPT Capabilities:**
- Structures test sessions and observation sheets
- Summarizes findings into insights
- Suggests next steps for iteration or scaling

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of the 5-day sequence, participants will:
- Understand the end-to-end Design Thinking process
- Practice key tools (Empathy Maps, POVs, Ideation Frameworks, Prototyping, Testing)
- Develop a mindset of user-centered problem solving

## Customization Notes
Each GPT has:
- Custom instructions aligned with the day’s theme
- Curated prompts and examples for that stage
- Optional tone and persona settings (e.g., facilitator, coach, mentor)

If you want to modify them:
- Adjust the “Instructions” field to refine its role (e.g., “Act as a workshop facilitator…”)
- Update “Knowledge” sections to add templates, slides, or example outputs

## Suggested Use Cases
- Teaching design thinking in classrooms
- Guiding self-paced learners through the process
- Supporting team design sprints remotely
