# Learner Contracts

## Do you need to have learners sign agreements as part of their pathway?

Here are a few options for how to do this:

### Existing implementations to collect learner agreements:

- Learners are asked to agree to the **Umuzi Learner Handbook** as a required part of onboarding (since February 9, 2026). See more information here: [SOP: Learner & Explorer Onboarding Overview](#)
  
- Learners can also be asked to sign their **XPL Sign On Agreement** as well as **Gig Portfolio Builder 1** agreements within the onboarding process. This is turned on or off based on the cohort. See more here: [SOP: Learner & Explorer Onboarding Overview](#)

- Submit a Tech request to build another type of learner agreement into the onboarding process that can either be applied for all learners or for certain cohorts.

### Flexible options for collecting agreements:

If you want something more flexible or have to collect agreements last minute, the best way to do so is to collect agreements in **Google Classroom**.

1. Create an assignment in the learners' Google Classroom that they are already working in. You can add any information & description for this and link to the template PDF and put a deadline.
   > **Note:** We do NOT recommend creating a new Google Classroom just for the contract.

2. Ask them to sign and upload & submit by posting an announcement in the Google Classroom or through another communication method with the learners.

3. Learners can follow this video guide to submit.

Within Google Classroom, any teacher will easily be able to see who has submitted the agreement and who has not in order to follow up. In the example below, we can see that 1 learner has submitted their contract (called **Turned In** in Google Classroom speak) and 2 learners have not yet submitted (called **Assigned**).

As learners submit, at least one person should be reviewing the submitted files to make sure they are indeed signed agreements & acceptable. You can grade them to note if acceptable or not.

You will also be able to ask anyone who submits the wrong file to resubmit directly within Google Classroom. When you do this, you can grade it with a 0 and put a private comment to explain what is wrong and ask them to resubmit. See this demo video. Be sure that **Accepting Submissions** is turned on, so learners can resubmit.

Learners will then be able to see your comment & resubmit.

### Additional tips:

More tips & tricks for Google Classroom are available here: [Google Classroom](#)

- Send agreements out to learners in an email or Google Form. 
  > **Note:** This is not a recommended solution because it requires much more manual collection of the signed agreements and it's harder to track who has submitted it and who hasn't. You will need to compare lists of who has submitted with a list of all learners to see who has not submitted. Google Classroom does this for you automatically.
