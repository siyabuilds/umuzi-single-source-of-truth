# Slack Channel Naming Conventions

Slack works best when channels are easy to find and make sense at a glance. To keep things tidy (and save everyone from guessing games), we use a few simple prefixes when creating new channels. Each prefix gives a quick clue about what the channel’s for — whether it’s asking for help, planning an event, or running a project. This way, everyone knows where to go, and we avoid duplicate or confusing channel names.

Check out the list below for the prefixes we use and some examples to guide you when setting up a new channel.

## Prefixes and Examples

- **help-** To ask questions or find information from other teams or departments.  
  Examples: 
  - `#help-benefits`
  - `#help-finance`
  - `#help-it`

- **team-** For teams to coordinate work and activities.  
  Examples: 
  - `#team-design`
  - `#team-support`
  - `#team-sales`

- **proj-** For cross-functional teams working together on a specific project.  
  Examples: 
  - `#proj-redesign`
  - `#proj-office-move`
  - `#proj-expense-reports`

- **cohort-** For channels relating to specific cohorts of learners (unemployed or employed learnerships).  
  Example: 
  - `#cohort-c50-vml`

- **event-** To plan and execute events, large and small.  
  Examples: 
  - `#event-board-mtg`
  - `#event-company-picnic`
  - `#event-hr-offsite`

- **exp-** For an experiment.  
  Example: 
  - `#exp-learning-pods`

- **temp-** Temporary channels on specific topics, to be archived once resolved.  
  Example: 
  - `#temp-nerd-alignment`

- **partner-** For channels relating to specific partners.  
  Example: 
  - `#partner-bbd`

- **ops-** For channels relating to Umuzi operations.  
  Example: 
  - `#ops-website`

- **proposal-** For channels relating to a specific proposal.  
  Example: 
  - `#proposal-jobs-fund`
