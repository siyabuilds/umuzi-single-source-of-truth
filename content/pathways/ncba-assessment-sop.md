# NCBA Assessment SOP

## How do I assess the submissions?

Hi, so you are preparing to facilitate your first NCBA sprint and are wondering how you should assess the learners. Well, let's go through that together.

To start, please ensure that you went through this **Cohort Onboarding Guideline for NCBA Slab** which explains everything to do with the NCBA course and assessment structure. If you have done so, let's move to the next step.

## How will the learners be assessed?

The linked Slab above makes it clear that there are two assessments for each module. These are:

1. **Formative Assessments**
2. **Summative Assessments**

### Formative Assessments

The formative assessment is taken by the cohort on the Mini extension portal and they are assessed automatically on Airtable. Once they submit their answers to the multiple-choice questions, they will be able to see their scores immediately on the learning portal. So this means you don't have to assess this assessment type yourself, yay!

However, the formative assessment may include open-ended answers and sometimes deliverables, which will need to be assessed by a human with a clear rubric and understanding of context. Part of the MICSETA requirement is that the submission for this course is assessed by an accredited assessor, and we have one: **Dr. Augustine**.

### How do you request for this service?

There is a process that must be employed when reaching out. You will need to collaborate with **Thabile Nhlapho** to organize a new cohort onboarding, where the assessor will meet with the cohort likely on the same day or the same week as their course onboarding. Once the assessor is done with the MICSETA assessment onboarding, you will need to organize a session where you will clearly indicate to them the assessment schedule. This is when you will need a summative assessment to be marked. You can use this spreadsheet as a template to set this up.

Once you have done this, the next step is to make sure you follow the schedule. This means that you need to ensure that the cohort is never late with their submissions.

> If they are ever late, you will need to communicate this over email with Thabile and Dr. Augustine.
