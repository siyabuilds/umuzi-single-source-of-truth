# Grow with Google: Project Management Assessment SOP

This is the standard operating procedure when managing cohorts registered on the Grow with Google (GwG) Project Management course.

## Assessing the GwG PM Course

The GwG Project Management course will be facilitated on the Coursera online self-paced learning platform.

## Schedule

The course is estimated to be **124 hours** of course content. Though it is self-paced, we do have a course schedule to ensure that cohorts maintain the same progress for assessment and course review purposes.

We make use of a Google Sheet template to help the cohorts visualize their learning journey on GwG and also plan their work timeline and effort accordingly. The template takes into account the total hours required to cross the weeks in each of the courses, so the milestones for progress will be measured in weeks per course. 

The scheduling and progress document requires that the cohorts commit to reaching milestones within the agreed-upon end date. So the first requirement will be that the cohorts taking the course should adhere to the course completion timeline.

### Recommendations for Schedules

The course offering consists of **124 hours** of a compilation of:
- Reading material
- Practice exercises and quizzes
- Weekly graded quizzes
- Peer graded assignments
- Peer assignment grading

The total course duration is divided by the average working hours in a week (40), and the estimated time for completion would be **3 and a half weeks** for full-time cohorts. Considering the cohort wellness will only demand from them six hours of work from their nine-hour day.

They will therefore spend their 8-hour day in the following way:
- 45-minute focused sessions with 15 minutes in between * 6 (6 hours of work in total and 2 hours of rest).
- 1 hour of lunch after the first 4 hours.

If they can keep up with the schedule, then the cohorts will be considered to be doing well on time management and project scheduling. Should a cohort struggle with the timeline, they should make clear their impediments during the weekly stand-up sessions. This should also allow us an opportunity to intervene with support earlier on the course timeline and possibly involve Rivoningo from the Learner Support Dept for Telkom.

## Course Content Retention

The GwG course setup already assesses the cohorts quite well; however, we need to be able to measure their retention of the course content. The content retention will be assessed in **3 ways**:
1. Weekly revision session
2. At the beginning of the week when we finish a course, we will assign topics to either a group or individuals (depending on the cohort group size). Please see the list of topics per course.
3. A brief is to be completed at the end of the course where the cohorts will be required to give an application reflection on any 4 topics of their choice. The brief will be assessed with this rubric.
