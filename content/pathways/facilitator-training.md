# Facilitator Training

## AIM
To ensure that engagements between learners and facilitators are informative, professional, and useful. Providing training to facilitators in content delivery, communication, and professionalism has been identified as a method of achieving this outcome.

## PHASE 1
The first phase of training for facilitators will focus on foundational skills such as speaking, engaging in a group, and leading sessions. Below is a list of recommended online courses which cover these skills. It is advised that they are completed sequentially as the skills build on top of each course.

### Courses
- **Facilitation: You Can Be a Facilitator**  
  Link to course: [Udemy Course](https://www.udemy.com/course/how-to-be-a-facilatator/)  
  Duration: 3 weeks at 2 hours per day

- **Instructional Design: Facilitation Skills**  
  Link to course: [FutureLearn Course](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/facilitation-skills)  
  Duration: 1.5 weeks at 1 hour per day

- **How To Teach Online: Providing Continuity for Students**  
  Link to course: [FutureLearn Course](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/teach-online)  
  Duration: 1 week at 1.5 hours per day

### Rollout Sessions
This is a proposed schedule for the rollout of the first phase:
1. Introduction to the first phase and the online courses and identified value
2. Complete online courses
3. Practical practice - applying skills learned
4. Reflection of learnings and insights
5. Session visits - manager joining sessions to observe and take notes
6. Weekly check-in

## PHASE 2
The second phase of training for facilitators will focus on advanced facilitation and leadership skills such as stakeholder management, conflict resolution, and negotiation.
