# Moderation

Moderation is the process of ensuring that the Assessment Process is conducted according to predefined standards and is **fair**, **valid**, and **reliable**. The moderation process starts once the assessment has been completed.

## Why do we moderate?

- To ensure that assessments are fair, valid, reliable, and practical.
- To evaluate the performance of assessors.
- To ensure that quality standards of assessments are maintained.
- To provide feedback to improve assessment practice and methods.
- To provide an opportunity for dissatisfied learners to appeal the assessment outcomes.
- To make recommendations for the reassessment of learners.
- To provide a procedure for the de-registration/re-registration of assessors.
- To provide feedback to relevant stakeholders.

## To moderate an assessment

Another assessor should perform an assessment on a submission that has already been assessed. The same steps for the first assessment should be followed, and allocated marks can be adjusted to ensure that the marks are fair, valid, and reliable.
