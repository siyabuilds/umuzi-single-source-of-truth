## FACILITATOR - ROLE
The role of the facilitator is to guide and mentor the learners through the [course component]. At Umuzi, our facilitation approach is to enable and empower learners to learn for themselves and from each other. We focus on encouraging them to think critically about their context, the concepts, and skills we're sharing and assist them with any blockers.

We run our course in an agile, sprint way which allows learners to focus on specific skills and tasks at hand. The following mechanics are in place to assist with this:
- **Briefing Sessions** - these are sessions focused on information sharing, facilitated by the Head of DPD.
- **Stand Ups** - these are status checks for progress updates and blockers. These will be run independently by each facilitator with approximately 20 learners, which will then be divided into smaller teams.
- **Reviews** - these are sessions to give the learners feedback on the work they've completed that day.

These sessions will be allocated in your calendar. However, the learners may have blockers outside of this time when you may need to assist. See the Communication section below.

## COMMUNICATION
There will be separate WhatsApp Groups for each group and for us internally - this will help us manage any blockers that you or the learners may have.

We will communicate acceptable conduct and expected behaviour with the learners in the onboarding session. The following is communicated:

> "Once we begin MUB you will be assigned a group and a Facilitator, which you will be able to communicate with on the Group WhatsApp regarding content-related queries or blockers you might have. This will ensure that you have a clear line of communication constantly running with your group and that any queries you have are shared on the group because often if one person is confused, a few more might be!  
> We ask that you treat these WhatsApp groups with respect and communicate in a professional manner, respecting all people in the group.  
> Your facilitators are available during the allocated time for the morning stand-up and afternoon reviews; they will intermittently check in with the WhatsApp group in between these times to ensure there are no emergencies. We encourage you to work independently as a team gaining as much socially contextual knowledge as possible during this time.  
> We encourage you to act professionally and courteously and foster a curious and respectful environment for all."

## FACILITATION TIPS
### Starting a session
It’s always great to start a session by introducing yourself, your experience, and your passion for the field you're in. It’s a great way to set the tone of the session and establish trust. 

Ice Breakers are also a great way to help people get to know each other - “2 Truths and a Lie” is always a win!

### Learner Feedback
Facilitating online can be tricky and difficult to gauge how learners are doing or if they understand the concepts that are being communicated. Here are some solutions for if you are ever unsure as to how the group is receiving your content:
- Ask them to message in the chat a number between 1 - 5 (1 = very confused, 5 = very clear).
- Ask them to message in the chat an emoji reflecting how they feel or how well they understand your content.

This really helps to gauge the group and energy levels.
